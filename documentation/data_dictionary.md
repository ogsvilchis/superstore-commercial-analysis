# Data Dictionary
## Superstore Commercial Strategy Analysis

**Source file:** superstore_raw.csv  
**Cleaned file:** superstore_cleaned.xlsx  
**Total columns:** 26 (21 original + 5 calculated)  
**Total records:** 9,994  
**Date range:** January 3, 2014 – December 30, 2017  

---

## Original Columns

| # | Column Name | Data Type | Source | Description | Example | Business Rule | Notes |
|---|-------------|-----------|--------|-------------|---------|---------------|-------|
| 1 | Row_ID | Integer | Original | Unique identifier for each row in the dataset | 1 | Must be unique across all records. No business meaning beyond row identification | Verified — no duplicates found |
| 2 | Order_ID | Text | Original | Unique identifier for each customer order | CA-2014-152156 | Format: XX-YYYY-NNNNNN. Multiple rows can share the same Order ID when an order contains multiple products | Not a true unique key at row level |
| 3 | Order_Date | Date | Original — Fixed | Date when the order was placed by the customer | 01/03/2014 | Format corrected from mixed text/serial issues caused by regional settings conflict. Range: Jan 3 2014 – Dec 30 2017 | Fixed using IF(ISNUMBER()) + FIND("/") formula. See Cleaning_Log row 2 |
| 4 | Ship_Date | Date | Original — Fixed | Date when the order was shipped to the customer | 07/01/2014 | Always equal to or later than Order Date. Range: Jan 7 2014 – Jan 5 2018 | Fixed using IF(ISNUMBER()) + FIND("/") formula. See Cleaning_Log row 3 |
| 5 | Ship_Mode | Text | Original | Shipping method selected by the customer | Second Class | 4 values: First Class, Second Class, Standard Class, Same Day | Relevant for logistics cost analysis |
| 6 | Customer_ID | Text | Original | Unique identifier for each customer | CG-12520 | A single Customer ID can appear in multiple orders across different dates | Not unique at row level |
| 7 | Customer_Name | Text | Original | Full name of the customer | Claire Gute | For reference only — Customer ID is the reliable identifier for analysis | Do not use as join key |
| 8 | Segment | Text | Original | Business segment the customer belongs to | Consumer | 3 values: Consumer, Corporate, Home Office | Key dimension for customer segmentation analysis |
| 9 | Country | Text | Original | Country of the customer | United States | All 9,994 records are United States — single-country dataset | No geographic variation at country level |
| 10 | City | Text | Original | City of the customer | Henderson | 531 unique cities across the dataset | High granularity — use State or Region for aggregated analysis |
| 11 | State | Text | Original | State of the customer | Kentucky | 49 unique states represented | Key geographic dimension for regional analysis |
| 12 | Postal_Code | Text | Original — Fixed | 5-digit US ZIP code of the customer | 42420 | Must be stored as Text to preserve leading zeros. Fixed using TEXT(value,"00000") | Leading zeros were lost during import — corrected. See Cleaning_Log row 4 |
| 13 | Region | Text | Original | Geographic sales region | South | 4 values: East, West, Central, South | Primary geographic dimension for executive reporting |
| 14 | Product_ID | Text | Original | Unique identifier for each product | FUR-BO-10001798 | Format: CAT-SUBCAT-NNNNNNNNN. A product can appear in multiple orders | Use for product-level analysis and joins |
| 15 | Category | Text | Original | High-level product category | Furniture | 3 values: Furniture, Office Supplies, Technology | Primary product dimension for portfolio analysis |
| 16 | Sub_Category | Text | Original | Product sub-category within each category | Bookcases | 17 unique sub-categories across 3 categories | Key dimension for profitability analysis by product type |
| 17 | Product_Name | Text | Original | Full name of the product | Bush Somerset Collection Bookcase | Most granular product dimension | High cardinality — use Sub-Category or Category for aggregations |
| 18 | Sales | Decimal | Original | Revenue generated by the order line in USD | 261.96 | Always positive. Range: $0.44 – $22,638.48. Represents gross revenue before costs | Does not represent profit — always analyze alongside Profit and Discount |
| 19 | Quantity | Integer | Original | Number of units ordered | 2 | Always positive integer. No zero or negative values found | — |
| 20 | Discount | Decimal | Original | Discount rate applied to the order line | 0.20 | Stored as decimal between 0 and 1. Example: 0.20 = 20% discount. Range: 0.00 – 0.80 | High discounts strongly correlate with negative profit — key finding |
| 21 | Profit | Decimal | Original | Net profit or loss generated by the order line in USD | 41.91 | Can be negative — represents loss-making orders. Range: -$6,599.98 – $8,399.98. 1,936 records show Profit ≤ 0 | Negative profit is valid business data — do not remove |

## Calculated Columns

| # | Column Name | Data Type | Source | Description | Example | Business Rule | Notes |
|---|-------------|-----------|--------|-------------|---------|---------------|-------|
| 22 | Profit_Margin | Decimal | Calculated | Net profit expressed as a percentage of sales revenue | 0.16 | Formula: Profit / Sales. Can be negative. Range: -275% – +50% | Extreme minimum of -275% flagged for investigation in SQL. See Validation_Checks row 3 |
| 23 | Discount_Category | Text | Calculated | Business-friendly tier classification of the discount applied | Low | No Discount = 0.0 / Low = 0.01–0.20 / Medium = 0.21–0.40 / High = 0.41–0.80 | Thresholds based on standard retail discount tiers. Defined by analyst — can be adjusted by business stakeholders |
| 24 | Order_Year | Integer | Calculated | Calendar year extracted from Order Date | 2017 | Formula: YEAR(Order_Date). Values: 2014, 2015, 2016, 2017 | Used for annual trend and YoY growth analysis |
| 25 | Order_Month | Integer | Calculated | Calendar month extracted from Order Date | 11 | Formula: MONTH(Order_Date). Values: 1–12 stored as integers for correct sorting | Month name applied at visualization layer in Power BI — not stored as text |
| 26 | Is_Profitable | Text | Calculated | Flag indicating whether the order line generated positive profit | Yes | Formula: IF(Profit > 0, "Yes", "No"). No = Profit ≤ 0 — includes both negative and zero-profit orders. 1,936 records flagged as "No" (19.4%) | Profit = 0 classified as Not Profitable — zero margin orders still consume operational resources |
